A FOGs Garden

The Rock Garden

Established in 1996, tended by volunteers for nearly three decades — and now being thoughtfully restored.

History

The St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden lies between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses. Established in 1996 and tended by FOGs volunteers ever since, it is one of the longest-running volunteer-led garden projects on Western’s campus.

Restoration

The garden is currently being actively restored to align with today’s stewardship values. Volunteers help remove invasive ivy, clear overgrowth, protect rock features, and prepare the area for future native plantings. The work is seasonal and hands-on — no experience required.

Volunteer in the garden

Join us for a planting or restoration day — all experience levels welcome. Fill in our intake form and we’ll let you know when we’re next in the rock garden.
