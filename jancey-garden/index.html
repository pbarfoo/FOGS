A FOGs Garden

The Jancey Garden

“The planning and planting of the Jancey Garden allowed members to give vent to their creativity.”

History

The Jancey Memorial Garden was created by FOGs members at the request of then-Botany Chair and professor Alan Day, in honour of late Plant Sciences professor Robert Jancey. The project gave FOGs members an opportunity to express their horticultural creativity on a larger canvas than the rock garden alone.

In addition to planting some of their own favourite plants, shrubs, and small trees, members constructed a path through the garden. Picnic tables and memorial benches were installed, making it a place for the Western community to sit and enjoy.

The New Garden

The original Jancey Garden was later removed to make way for the construction of the Biotron — Western’s large-scale climate simulation facility. A new Jancey Garden, incorporating a number of the original plants, was created at the entrance to the Biotron, preserving the spirit of the memorial in a new location on campus.

The original garden through its four seasons was recorded and preserved on disc in the Western Archives, thanks to the efforts of former FOGs member Bruce McCoy.

Visit the garden

The Jancey Garden is located at the entrance to the Biotron on Western University’s campus. The plant sale is held nearby, between the Jancey Garden and the tropical greenhouses.
