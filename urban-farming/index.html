Urban Farming

Growing food, building community, and learning together — one plot at a time.

About the Project

FOGs stewards three community garden plots plus a plot within the new research garden on Western’s campus. These plots are used for growing fresh produce and hands-on urban agriculture education. Volunteers plant, tend, and harvest throughout the growing season, donating fresh produce to campus and city-wide food banks.

Get Involved

Volunteers support planting, watering, harvesting, and seasonal maintenance. Tasks are accessible to all experience levels — whether you’ve grown plants before or are just starting out. It’s a great way to learn about food systems, urban agriculture, and sustainable growing in a practical, hands-on setting.

Help us tend the plots and grow food for the community. Fill in our intake form to get connected.
