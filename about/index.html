About FOGs

Who We Are

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) is a volunteer group at Western University made up of staff, faculty, students, and community members who share a love of horticulture. Together, we maintain and beautify the gardens on Western's campus in London, Ontario, Canada.

Our group has been quietly and colourfully growing for nearly 30 years. FOGs was founded when a dedicated community member noticed that the St. Mary's Cement Rock Garden could use a little attention — and that small spark of initiative grew into the thriving volunteer community we are today.

The St. Mary's Cement Rock Garden

FOGs volunteers primarily work in the St. Mary's Cement Rock Garden, which lies between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching down from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses on Western's campus.

The rock garden is a beloved feature of Western's landscape, showcasing a wide variety of alpine and rock-loving plants maintained through the dedicated efforts of our volunteers.

The Pollinator Garden

In addition to the rock garden, FOGs volunteers also tend the Pollinator Garden on campus — a green space designed to support bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects. This garden reflects our commitment to sustainable horticulture and biodiversity on the Western campus.

What We Do

Our volunteers come together regularly to care for the gardens and share their horticultural knowledge. Activities include:

  • Weeding, planting, and general garden maintenance
  • Propagating plants for our annual plant sale
  • Hosting periodic workshops on topics such as:
    • Making troughs
    • Pruning techniques
    • Taking cuttings
    • Grafting
    • Plant care and identification
  • Sharing expertise with visitors and plant sale customers

Community Impact

Proceeds from our annual plant sale go towards supporting a community program and providing bursaries for students at Western University. Our work benefits not only the campus landscape but also the broader London community.

Want to get involved? We welcome new volunteers from the Western community and beyond. Contact us at info@fogsuwo.ca to learn more.
