About

Nearly 30 years of community gardening

“She noticed the rock garden could use a bit of work. That was 27 years ago, and their work has quietly and colourfully grown.”

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) is a group of volunteer gardeners at Western University. Volunteers include undergraduate students, alumni, staff, and retired staff from across the campus — as well as members of the broader London community. FOGs was founded by Frances Howey, BA’60, who retired from Western’s advancement services and turned her attention to the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden on campus. Over the years, FOGs has raised more than $50,000 in bursaries to support undergraduate science students at Western.

Our Gardens

The Rock Garden

FOGs volunteers work in the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden, which lies between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching down from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses. It is home to a wide range of alpine and rock-loving plants propagated and cared for by our members.

The Pollinator Garden

FOGs also tends the Western University Pollinator Garden — a dedicated green space for bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects. The garden reflects our broader commitment to biodiversity and sustainable horticulture on campus.

The Jancey Garden

The Jancey Memorial Garden was created by FOGs members at the request of then-Botany Chair Alan Day, in honour of late Plant Sciences professor Robert Jancey. The original garden was later removed to make way for the Biotron; a new Jancey Garden, incorporating some of the original plants, was created at the Biotron’s entrance.

Workshops

FOGs mostly learns by doing. Periodic workshops are held on topics such as making troughs, pruning, taking cuttings, grafting, and plant identification. All levels of experience are welcome.

Field Trips & Social

Field Trips

Past field trips have included rescuing plants from areas under construction, travelling to public gardens to obtain plants and find inspiration, and visiting the private gardens of fellow FOGs members and friends. Members are encouraged to show off their own gardens too.

Get-togethers

Occasionally FOGs holds social get-togethers, often combined with a business meeting. These give members the opportunity to meet like-minded people, swap plants, and share stories.

Volunteer with FOGs

Undergraduate students, alumni, Western staff and retired staff, and community members are all welcome. Get in touch to find out when we next meet in the garden.
