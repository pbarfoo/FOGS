About

Nearly 30 years of community gardening

“She noticed the rock garden could use a bit of work. That was 27 years ago, and their work has quietly and colourfully grown.”

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) is a volunteer-led gardening and sustainability organization, founded in 1994 by Frances Howey, BA’60. Volunteers include undergraduate students, alumni, staff, faculty, and retired staff from across the Western University campus — as well as members of the broader London community. Over the years, FOGs has raised more than $50,000 in bursaries to support science students at Western.

Our Mission

To care for Western’s green spaces through community-powered gardening that teaches, inspires, supports biodiversity, and land stewardship.

As a Group, FOGs Activities Are

To Work

FOGs volunteers tend the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building. Established in 1996, it is now being actively restored — volunteers help remove invasive ivy, clear overgrowth, protect rock features, and prepare beds for future native plantings.

To Learn

FOGs mostly learns by doing. Hands-on workshops cover topics including caring for houseplants, growing native plants at home, starting vegetables and herbs from seed, and crafts using plant materials. Volunteers also gain experience in propagation, seed collection, plant identification, and pest monitoring.

Past field trips include rescuing plants in areas under construction, visiting public gardens, and exploring fellow members’ gardens. Members are always encouraged to show off their own.

To Share

Occasionally there are FOGs get-togethers, often combined with a business meeting. This gives FOGs opportunities to meet and mingle with other like-minded people and swap plants and stories.

FOGs also participates in campus and community sustainability events, hosting booths, selling plants, and partnering with student and volunteer groups across campus.

Urban Farming

FOGs stewards three community garden plots plus a plot within the new research garden, growing fresh produce for the food bank and hands-on urban agriculture education. Volunteers support planting, harvesting, and seasonal maintenance, donating fresh produce to campus and city-wide food banks.

The Jancey Garden

The Jancey Memorial Garden was created by FOGs members at the request of then-Botany Chair Alan Day, in honour of late Plant Sciences professor Robert Jancey. The original garden was later removed to make way for the Biotron; a new Jancey Garden, incorporating some of the original plants, was created in the inner courtyard of the Biological & Geological Sciences Building.
