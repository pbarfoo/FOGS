About FOGs

Nearly 30 years of community gardening at the heart of Western University

Our Story

How It All Started

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) was founded when a dedicated community member — Frances Howey, BA'60 — retired from working in advancement services and noticed the St. Mary's Cement Rock Garden "could use a bit of work." That small spark of initiative, nearly 30 years ago, grew into the thriving volunteer community we are today.

FOGs is made up of Western staff, faculty, students, and community members who share a genuine love of horticulture. Together, we quietly and colourfully tend some of the most distinctive garden spaces on campus.

🪨

The Rock Garden

Our home base: the St. Mary's Cement Rock Garden, nestled between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching down from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses.

🐝

The Pollinator Garden

FOGs volunteers also maintain the Western University Pollinator Garden — a space designed to support bees, butterflies, and other vital insects, reflecting our commitment to biodiversity on campus.

📚

Workshops & Learning

We host periodic workshops on topics including making troughs, pruning, taking cuttings, grafting, plant identification, and general plant care. All experience levels are welcome.

What We Do

Our Activities

Volunteers gather regularly for garden maintenance and to share knowledge. Activities include:

  • Weeding, planting, and seasonal garden care
  • Propagating plants for the annual sale
  • Hosting horticultural workshops open to the community
  • Sharing expertise with plant sale visitors
  • Supporting Western University's sustainability and green space goals

Community Impact

Proceeds from our annual plant sale support a community program and provide bursaries for students at Western University. The gardens we tend enrich campus life for thousands of students, staff, and visitors each year.
