About

Nearly 30 years of community gardening

“She noticed the rock garden could use a bit of work. That was 27 years ago, and their work has quietly and colourfully grown.”

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) is a group of volunteer gardeners at Western University. Volunteers include undergraduate students, alumni, staff, and retired staff from across the campus — as well as members of the broader London community. FOGs was founded by Frances Howey, BA’60, who retired from Western’s advancement services and turned her attention to the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden on campus. Over the years, FOGs has raised more than $50,000 in bursaries to support undergraduate science students at Western.

As a Group, FOGs Activities Are

To Work

FOGs volunteers work in the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden that lies between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching down from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses.

To Learn

FOGs mostly learns by doing! Periodic workshops are held on topics such as making troughs, pruning, taking cuttings, grafting, taking care of plants, and plant identification.

Past field trips include rescuing plants in areas under construction, travelling to public gardens to obtain plants and be inspired, and visiting gardens of fellow FOGs members or friends. Members are encouraged to show off their own gardens.

To Share

Occasionally there are FOGs get-togethers, often combined with a business meeting. This gives FOGs opportunities to meet and mingle with other like-minded people and swap plants and stories.

FOGs volunteers also impart their expertise on visitors during the annual plant sale — through the plants they sell and their knowledge on how to care for them!

The Jancey Garden

The Jancey Memorial Garden was created by FOGs members at the request of then-Botany Chair Alan Day, in honour of late Plant Sciences professor Robert Jancey. The original garden was later removed to make way for the Biotron; a new Jancey Garden, incorporating some of the original plants, was created in the inner courtyard of the Biological & Geological Sciences Building.
