About

Nearly 30 years of community gardening

“She noticed the rock garden could use a bit of work. That was 27 years ago, and their work has quietly and colourfully grown.”

Friends of the Gardens (FOGs) was founded by Frances Howey, BA’60, who retired from Western’s advancement services and turned her attention to the St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden on campus. What began as one volunteer’s initiative has grown into a community of Western staff, faculty, students, and neighbours who share a genuine love of plants.

Our Gardens

The Rock Garden

The St. Mary’s Cement Rock Garden lies between the Collip Building and the Biological & Geological Sciences Building, stretching down from Middlesex Drive to the lower greenhouses. It is the heart of FOGs activity, home to a wide range of alpine, rock-loving, and shade plants propagated and cared for by our volunteers.

The Pollinator Garden

FOGs also tends the Western University Pollinator Garden, a dedicated green space for bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects. The garden reflects our broader commitment to biodiversity and sustainable horticulture on campus.

Workshops

We host periodic workshops open to members and friends. Topics have included making hypertufa troughs, pruning, taking cuttings, grafting, and plant identification. All levels of experience are welcome.

The Plant Sale

Our annual sale is a highlight of the Western calendar. Every plant is propagated by FOGs volunteers, and our knowledgeable team is always on hand to advise on care and placement. Proceeds support student bursaries and a community program.

Volunteer with FOGs

Western staff, students, faculty, and community members are all welcome. Get in touch to find out when we next meet in the garden.
